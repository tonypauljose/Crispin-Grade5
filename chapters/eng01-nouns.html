📖 English · Chapter 1 · Nouns

Grade 5 · CBSE · Learn about all the different kinds of nouns — and spot them like a pro.

1 What is a noun? ▼

A noun is a naming word. It names a person, place, animal, thing, or idea.

Examples:
Person: Crispin, teacher, doctor, friend
Place: Bahrain, school, park, stadium
Animal: dog, lion, fish, eagle
Thing: ball, book, pencil, phone
Idea/feeling: love, honesty, freedom, fear
💡 Tip: If a word names something, it's a noun. If it's an action (like "run"), it's a verb. If it describes (like "happy"), it's an adjective.
2 Common & Proper Nouns ▼

A common noun is a general name. A proper noun is the specific name of a person, place, thing or day.

CommonProper
boyCrispin
countryBahrain
monthApril
playerRonaldo
riverGanges
⚠️ Rule: Proper nouns always start with a CAPITAL letter — anywhere they appear in a sentence.
3 Singular & Plural Nouns (the regular rules) ▼

Singular = one. Plural = more than one.

RuleExample
Usually add -scat → cats · book → books
Ends in -s, -x, -sh, -ch: add -esbus → buses · box → boxes · dish → dishes · watch → watches
Consonant + y: change to -iesbaby → babies · story → stories
Vowel + y: just add -sboy → boys · day → days
Ends in -o (some): add -estomato → tomatoes · hero → heroes

5 Countable & Uncountable Nouns ▼

Countable nouns can be counted: 1 apple, 2 apples, 10 apples. Uncountable nouns can't — we use measures.

CountableUncountable
apple, chair, bookwater, milk, sugar, rice
friend, car, pencilfurniture, luggage, information, advice
"three apples""a glass of water", "a piece of information"
⚠️ Don't say "two milks" or "three informations". Say "two glasses of milk" and "three pieces of information".

7 Abstract & Concrete Nouns ▼

Concrete nouns name things you can see or touch.
Abstract nouns name feelings, qualities or ideas — things you can't touch.

ConcreteAbstract
ball, desk, dog, pencillove, fear, honesty, courage
water, book, phonefreedom, beauty, happiness
Crispin, Bahrainfriendship, anger, truth
💡 Quick test: Can you touch it? Yes → concrete. No → abstract.
8 Gender of Nouns ▼

Nouns can be masculine (male), feminine (female), neuter (things without gender) or common (can be either).

MasculineFeminine
kingqueen
princeprincess
uncleaunt
nephewniece
lionlioness
bullcow
stallionmare
wizardwitch
actoractress
waiterwaitress
cock (rooster)hen

Common gender: teacher, doctor, friend, student, cousin, baby — can be male or female.
Neuter: book, stone, table, car — things without gender.

9 Possessive Nouns (who owns what?) ▼

To show something belongs to someone, we use an apostrophe.

SituationRuleExample
Singular nounAdd 'sCrispin's book · the dog's tail · Sara's pen
Plural ending in sAdd only 'the boys' shoes · the players' kits
Plural NOT ending in sAdd 'sthe children's toys · the women's shoes · the men's team
⚠️ Common mistake: don't write "childrens'" — "children" doesn't end in s, so it's children's.
